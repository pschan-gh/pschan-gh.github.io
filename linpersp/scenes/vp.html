Let $\vec{n}_0$ be a normal vector of the Picture Plane. Let $L$ be a non-orthogonal (to the Picture Plane) line parallel to a nonzero vector $\vec{v}$ There is a unique plane $\mathcal{P}$ containing $L$ and perpendicular to the Picture Plane. It has a normal vector defined as follows: \[ \vec{n} = \vec{v} \times \vec{n}_0. \] This is the ground plane associated with the line.
(Under this framework, orthogonal lines have no grounds.)

Let $G$ be the line which is the intersection of $\mathcal{P}$ and the Picture Plane. We call it the ground line of $L$.

This plane has a vanishing line $H$, which is the unique line on the Picture Plane which passes through the center point $C$ and is parallel to $G$. We call this line $H$ the horizontal of $L$.

Two lines which has the same horizontal are said to be co-horizontal.

The vanishing points of all co-horizontal lines lie on the same horizontal on the Picture Plane.
